In the second book in this action-packed Midwestern pulp noir trilogy with shades of Elmore Leonard and Donald E. Westlake, ex-gangster Deuce Walsh quickly realizes that his week just went from bad to worse.The Chianti Brothers want Deuce to run one final job for them to repay what they consider to be a debt that he owes them,and in order to finish it Deuce has only one choice: to unite his former crew, all of whom think he's been dead for seven years."A fun pulp joyride."—Victor Gischler, author of Stay and Gun Monkeys“A worthy successor to Scratch the Surface, and Stevens shows himself to be an author not just content to take the easy choices, but ready and able to build upon what has gone before.”—Crime Fiction Lover
