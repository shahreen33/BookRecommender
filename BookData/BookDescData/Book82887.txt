From Mary Frances Auferheide, a musician and ragtime composer, to Virginia Jenckes, the first Indiana woman to be elected to Congress, and Gene Stratton, a photographer and author who published numerous novels based on her Indiana experiences, and Harriet Thorpe, taken captive as a child from the Whitewater River Valley near Richmond, Indiana, during the War of 1812, the history of Indiana women is varied and fascinating. This book reveals the lives of twelve remarkable Indiana women.
