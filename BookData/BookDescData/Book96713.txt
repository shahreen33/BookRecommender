Ophelia is tired of being treated like a spoiled, little rich girl. She wants to be seen as a woman. But she never expects to collide with her housekeeper’s son on the day he’s released from prison. After seven years behind bars, Ezra can’t control himself around such a sweet, young thing like Ophelia. She’s claiming to be his mother’s neighbor and he senses she’s lying…but he’ll have to worry about the details later. After she’s been claimed.
