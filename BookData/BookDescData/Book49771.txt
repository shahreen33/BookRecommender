Every family seems to know at least one person with Parkinson’s, an ancient and complex disease that confounds treatments beyond vigorous exercise, challenging memory games and powerful medications. Here’s what happened to Joyce Saunders, the protagonist of this story. She became a genius for denial right after her family doctor told her that she probably had Parkinson’s. Disguising her symptoms, Joyce managed to avoid everyone around her. Attempting to salvage their marriage, Joyce and Martin took March Break in the Caribbean where they discovered more than they expected and barely made it home in one piece. This is a passionate, larger-than-life novel, one that tweaks your conscience and helps you address the challenges of Parkinson’s Disease in your life.
